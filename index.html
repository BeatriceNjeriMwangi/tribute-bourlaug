DR NORMAN BOURLAUG

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Bourlaug, third from left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields, part of his life life-long war on hunger.

Here is a timeline of Dr. Norman Bourlang

  • 1914 -Born in cresco, lowa
  • 1933 -Leaves his family's farm to attend the unversity of minnesota, thanks to a Despression era program known as the 'National Youth Administration'
  • 1935 -Has to stop school and save up more money.Work in the civilian conservation corps helping starving American."I saw how food changed them".he said."All of this left scars on me ."
  • 1937 -Finishes university and takes a job in the US forestry
  • 1938 -Marries wife of 69 years Margret Gidson. Get laid off due to budget cuts. Inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman, he returnd to school study under stakman ,who teaches him about breeding pest-resistent plants
  • 1941 -Tries to enroll in the military after the pearl harbor attack,but is rejected.Instead, the military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue,DDT to control malaria , disenfectants, and other applied science
  • 1942 -Recieved a Ph.D. on Genetics and plant pathology
  • 1944 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pregnant wife, and flies to mexico to head a new plant pathology program. Over the next 16 years, his team breeds 6,000 different strains of disease resistent wheat - includes different varieties for each major climate on Earth
  • 1945 -Discovers a way to grown wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yields
  • 1953 -Crosses a short ,study dwarf breed of wheat with a high-yieldling American breed,creating a strain that responds well to fertalizer.It goes on to provide 95% of Mexico's wheat
  • 1962 -Vist Delhi and bring his high miltigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population
  • 1970 -receives the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 -helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields
  • 2005 -states "we will have to double the world food supply by 2050."Argues that genetically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demands, as we run out arable land .Says that GM crop are not inherently dangerous because "we've been genetically modifying plantsb and animals for a long time . Long before we called science, people were selecting the best breeds."
  • 2009 -dies at the age of 95.
